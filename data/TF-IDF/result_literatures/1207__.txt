NASICON-type Na3V2(PO4)3 has recently been investigated as a prospective cathode material for Na-ion batteries.15,16 The electrochemical response of this kind of cathode exhibits two potential plateaus located at 3.4 V and 1.6 V vs. Na/Na+ related to the V3+/V4+ and V2+/V3+ redox couples, respectively, and the  1 theoretical capacity varies between 117 and 236 mA h g depending on the cycling potential and the variation between V3+/V4+ and V2+/V3+ redox states.15 The low potential plateau exhibited by this material makes it a candidate for an anode in rechargeable batteries. However, the poor electronic conductivity of phosphates makes it diÔ¨Écult to achieve capacities close to the theoretical values in this NASCION-type cathode material. EÔ¨Écient strategies to overcome this shortcoming are mainly focused on providing electrically conductive coatings on particle surfaces and/or reducing particle sizes. To date, very few reports on Na3V2(PO4)3 prepared by solid-state and solution reactions are available, and the reported electrochemical performances of this phosphate-based Na-insertion host

generally demonstrate capacity limitations at charge‚Äìdischarge rates.17‚Äì19 Very reported porous Na3V2(PO4)/C zero-dimensional (0D) nanoparticle aggregations for Na-ion batteries with high cycling stability and superior rate capability.20 Subsequent research work on underscoring their application potential for Na-ion batteries encouraged us to develop new advanced methods to synthesise such high performance cathodes for future room-temperature Na-ion batteries. Herein, we adopt a versatile electrospinning method21‚Äì23 to synthesize a Na3V2(PO4)3/carbon electrode with a novel one-dimensional in which 0D Na3V2(PO4)3 nanoparticles were uniformly encapsulated by interconnecting 1D carbon nanoÓÑÉbers, denoted as [(Na3V2(PO4)3)0D/(carbon)1D]1D. Such a structure combines a variety of advantages for battery electrodes: very short diÔ¨Äusion length of Na ions in Na3V2(PO4)3, easy access of the organic electrolyte and the short transport path of Na+ through carbon toward phosphate nanoparticles, high conductivity transport of electrons along the percolation through ÓÑÉbers and avoidance of Ostwald ripening. introduction of separate Na3V2(PO4)3 particles, fully-encapsulated by carbon shells, minimizes the strain problems during (de-)insertion. Note that the volume shrinkage from Na3V2(PO4)3 to NaV2(PO4)3 is approximately 8.3%, which is similar to that of LiFePO4 (ca. 6.8%).18 Our electrochemical results demonstrated that a stable rate capability of NaV2(PO4)3/C could be ÓÑÉnally realized.

Na3V2(PO4)3 has a well-deÓÑÉned NASICON structure, with each primitive cell containing six Na3V2(PO4)3 formula units. The crystalline structure of Na3V2(PO4)3 is characterized by VO6 octahedra, each corner sharing with three PO4 tetrahedra, and by Na ions occupying the M1 (octahedral sites) and M2 (tetrahedral sites) sites of the interstitial sites. Future work must be carried out to optimize the detailed synthesis parameters to increase the surface area and porosity.

The development of high voltage plateau and capacity cathodes for Na-ion batteries is still in its incipient stages, and there is a great deal of future work required. In this study, a novel Na3V2(PO4)3/C electrode with a 1D nanostructure was synthesized for the ÓÑÉrst time using a simple electrospinning method. In the Na3V2(PO4)3/C cathode electrode, Na3V2(PO4)3 nanoparticles were well dispersed and conÓÑÉned in the center axis of the 1D carbon ÓÑÉbers. Such an electrode exhibited excellent cycling stability at high current rates. Its pronounced strong structural stability enabled it to withstand the severe volume change during 1D morphology connected with eÔ¨Écient electrochemical coupling and conductive network leads to superior rate capability, recommending its use in high power applications.
